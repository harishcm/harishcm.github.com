Learning Projects

Learning projects can be seen as analogous to modules within a formal education course. They serve as a means of consolidation and organisation. However, unlike modules, learning projects are meant to be flexible in methodology and unrestricted in scope.

Learning projects also need to cater for divergence from initial learning goals. Divergence is bound to occur, and is part of parcel of the discovery process.

Lifecycles of Learning Projects

Typical lifecycle of learning projects: Learning projects start off from ad-hoc interests. As they develop, they could be split into two or more branches. Learning projects could be left open ended or could end with a conclusion.
Alternate lifecycle of learning projects: Two or more ad-hoc interests that exhibit synergy could be combined into a single learning project. Two learning projects that have converged over time could be merged into a single learning project.

Learning projects begin naturally from one or more ad-hoc interests. For instance, there could be a certain synergy or commonality between two or more of your ad-hoc interests that you wish to investigate and build upon. At other times, you may have developed specific aims and goals regarding one of your ad-hoc interests, and wish to pursue them with greater vigor.

Learning projects then proceed with the self-directed learner locating and exploring relevant resources. He/she has to freedom to use resources and a learning methodology that suits him/her best. Special emphasis is placed upon documenting the investigative process behind uncovering information and relevant resources. Record keeping is considered vital.

Learning projects are generally not bounded in breadth or depth. Often however, the self-directed learner will realise that a different direction from the one currently pursued, is necessary. This is likely to happen after one stumbles upon one or more unknown unknowns. This is also likely to happen when one finds that prerequisite knowledge is necessary for effectively continuing with the current learning project. Sometimes, he/she may also find a learning project to have become to complex or diverse to manage.

At such conjunctures, the current learning project may be split into two or more branches. Alternatively, situations may arise when two or more learning projects are merged together.

Dormant learning projects are usually left open for input indefinitely. Partially, this is to symbolize that learning never ends. More importantly however, it is natural for learning to occur in leaps and bounds. Thus, it is common for one to go back to dormant learning projects months or years later. Well-kept records of the investigative process facilitate such situations.

Managing Divergence

Divergence refers to the tendency to get interested in several other things while pursuing a single learning interest. If not managed properly, one can be left with the feeling that one has not pursued anything to completion.

Divergence is a common experience and is part and parcel of the process of the discovery process. As you start learning about something, you become aware that there are so many other things that you never knew existed. These make you better informed. Yet at the same time they may be more interesting or more important to you. They may be prerequisites for your current learning project or may value add to it. Thus, for very good reasons, you will often get tempted to diverge from your current pursuits.

Two of ways of managing divergence are discussed below.

Setting moving targets instead of stationary ones

Setting moving targets instead of stationary ones provides you with an mindset that accepts that divergence is probably and welcomed. Firstly, such a positive mindset allows you to deal with divergence better. Secondly, it relaxes negative feelings associated with not able to see a particular, fixed, pursuit through to completion.

Branching off new ad-hoc interests and learning projects

With learning projects, you are free to branch off new ad-hoc interests and learning projects from those currently pursued. Then, you either archive records and creations from your current pursuit and start the new one, or you take record your new interest and continue with your current pursuit. Either way, you are fully aware that you can still go back to either one in the future, and can thus continue with a peace of mind.
