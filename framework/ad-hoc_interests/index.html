Ad-hoc Interests

Ad-hoc interests are the inspiring questions, ideas, interests, perspectives, realisations, etc. that arise in one’s mind from time to time. They may simply arise spontaneously or may develop over time, through certain experiences or because of livelihood issues.

Unfortunately, these very ad-hoc interests are often subject to self-suppression, right from their onset. Many a time, we feel that they cannot be important or that they may turn out to be just a waste of time. We sometimes also feel that other things (eg. school, work) should be allocated the time, effort and resources instead.

The case for ad-hoc interests

Though ad-hoc interests may seem small individually, collectively they define a large part of one’s individuality. They can also be starting points in identifying one’s true talents.

Pursuing one’s ad-hoc interests leads to expansion of one’s internal locus of control and extents of self-determination. When combined with small successes over time, pursuing one ad-hoc interests leads to personal empowerment.

Their link with self-directed learning

Being raw, personalized and readily available, ad-hoc interests make great starting points for self-directed learning. The excitement and sense of purpose that surrounds them can be tapped to spark and fuel learning pursuits.

Learning is thus initiated and developed intrinsically while resources and guidance from the outside are enthusiastically sought after.
