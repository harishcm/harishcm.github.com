Ad-hoc Interests

Ad-hoc interests are the inspiring questions, ideas, interests, perspectives, realisations, etc. that arise in your mind from time to time. They may simply arise spontaneously, develop over time, through certain experiences or because of livelihood issues.

Unfortunately, these very ad-hoc interests are often subject to self-suppression, right from their onset. Many a time, we feel that they cannot be important or that they may turn out to be just a waste of time. We sometimes also feel that other things (eg. school, work) should be allocated the time, effort and resources instead.

The case for ad-hoc interests

Though ad-hoc interests may seem small individually, collectively they define a large part of your individuality. They can also be starting points in identifying your true talents.

Pursuing your ad-hoc interests leads to expansion of your internal locus of control and extents of self-determination. When combined with small successes, over time, pursuing your ad-hoc interests leads to personal empowerment.

Their link with self-directed learning

Being raw, personalized and readily available, ad-hoc interests make great starting points for self-directed learning. The excitement and sense of purpose that surrounds them can be tapped to spark and fuel learning pursuits.

Learning is thus initiated and developed intrinsically while resources and guidance from the outside are enthusiastically sought after. When your ad-hoc interests become sufficiently developed, or sufficiently numerous, you may wish to organise them into one or more learning projects.
