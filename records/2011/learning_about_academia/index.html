Learning About Academia

  1. Inspiration and Reflection 2012-08-05

      I worked on a nature-environment related project in my final year at university, and was offered a post as Research Engineer by my supervisor. I took it up, and during my time in the position, I was seriously considering Research as a career.

      I was especially interested in the “social” and career aspects of research—in particular academia. What I found was astonishing:

      Research today is very much more varied and significantly deviant from the silhouettes of research commonly presented in mainstream media and discourse. On a darker side, there are crucial “social” (or “power relational”) and career aspects of research today that are rarely presented to and discussed with budding researchers. Research itself is a lot more about money, power relations and superficial publication numbers than what it is commonly believed to be.

      On the brighter side, there is increased awareness of these issues, and there is hope that transformation in entrepreneurship, globalisation, technology, economies, societies, education and research itself, will bring about solutions. Perhaps the problems themselves or research itself or the very people most affected by the nature of research today would bring about the solutions themselves.

      For the impatient, the issues can be reduced to the number of PhD graduates each year greatly exceeding the number of “good” research jobs created. To me however, a deeper understanding of the issues was an exciting endeavour. A deeper understanding of the issues is an endeavour that is also required of people rooting to reform the industry, the profession, and for research to remain “a calling” to budding academics.
