Singapore Issues

  1. Inspiration

      The Singapore General Election 2011 was revolutionary to me. It was the first time that I had voiced out and campaigned about a multitude of issues related to Singapore in the public sphere—the internet—without feeling a fear of prosecution or ridicule.

      It’s a new social movement in Singapore, and I am extremely glad for this new voice that I have found, and for the efforts of the many people who have helped in bringing it about.

      It now makes sense to have a separate learning project for my works and links to resources regarding Singapore issues.

    17. Singapore
      1. Cross-Roads of the East
      2. BY The Screen Traveller
      3. Short Film, 1938
      4. URL
      5. Perspective 2012-09-24

          A glimpse into parts of Singapore in the 1930s. It was amazing—the beauty of the architecture and the developed state of the roads and amenities. While only a small proportion of the population was able to make full use of them, it showcases the high degree of development that had already occurred in Singapore during the earlier part of the 20th century.

      6. Thought 2012-09-24

          I had seen this short film several months before, but kept thinking that the film couldn’t have been made in the 1930s. I kept thinking that the date was faked in some way. It was only upon viewing it again, and looking at the imagery at a deeper level, that I began to feel that it may actually be truly filmed in the 1930s.

          It is interesting that I had this mental model of a “backward” Singapore in the 1930s. Obviously, a large part had to do with our history textbooks in school, but something makes me feel that this is a lot deeper than that. It is something that is not confined to Singapore alone, but something that can shed light upon the ways in which we think about our forefathers, the past, and about history itself.
