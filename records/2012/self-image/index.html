Self-image

  1. Initial Inspiration

      It has been a long journey, along which I have dealt with several self-image issues.

      I am a stutterer, and it affects my self image, but the entire package is much, much larger than stuttering itself. A part of it is cultural, or rather feelings linked to cultural acceptance and an illusion of cultural superiority-inferiority.

      Self-image played a large part in me setting up this website in the first place, and in wanting to find a solution to my grievances against the formal education system I was in when I was younger.

      I dare to bring this up, now, as I now have more than a glimpse of Research—often touted to be the “highest calling of the academically trained mind”—which I had the opportunity to get into, largely due to learning skills I picked up along the way while being engaged in self-directed learning, and in part, due to my supervisor being impressed by this website itself.

      I am no longer afraid of being put down as less intelligent, or out of my league, as a I have seen and read about happenings in this “highest calling”, and have first hand experience that both hard work and passion are necessary in coming up with bold, cutting-edge ideas.

      I also recently recently made a career change, in a large part, to address my self-image. I think that this learning project will be a lot more intertwined with my life and personal circumstances—maybe somewhat more towards the spirit of self-directed learning in itself.

      I have had a lot of self-image issues in the past, but it is only now that I feel motivated and courageous enough to collate and share resources and experiences in the online world.
