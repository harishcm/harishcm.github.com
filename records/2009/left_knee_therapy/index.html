Left Knee Therapy

  1. Inspiration

      I sometimes have an prolonged disomfort in my left knee while walking or running. At times, it develops into a slight pain. It seems to be especially noticeable when I am walking/running on hard surfaces such as roads and pavements (which cover most of Singapore).

      I have had this on and off for maybe 8 years now, interspaced by periods with no noticeable discomfort. I feel that it may be a developmental flaw that may be corrected with good physiotherapy.

      Last year, I sought medical advice but ended up greatly dissatisfied with the handling of my case. First the GP did not refer me to a physiotherapist but instead to a bone doctor. Then, the bone doctors seemed to simply want to quickly glance over my case.

      The first one who requested me to take a x-ray of the knee cap only saw the image that gave a front view (anterior posterior), and said that my bones had no fracture cracks and that rest would solve it. Yes, I knew that as well, but I also knew that it was a temporary solution. I was angry that the didn’t even see the side view (lateral) images.

      At the second appointment, I had to point out to another bone doctor that the left knee cap seemed to be differently placed from the right knee cap. Again I was told that rest would probably solve my discomfort. When I pressed for an explanation he said that I may want to consider going for an MRI if I felt too troubled by the discomfort.

      At that point I did not want to continue with such medical advise any longer as I felt that the service rendered did not even substantiate the subsidised amount that I was paying. And my god, the government was paying the remainder, which was huge!

      I realised that I did not yet know enough to be able to make the medical fraternity work for me in helping me fix my problem. So I decided to first go and find out more about possible causes, explanations, and therapy, and experiement with them on my own.

  2. Knee joint

      Highly complex

    1. Bone
    2. Articular capsule
      1. Synovial membrane
      2. Fibrous membrane
      3. Fatty deposits
    3. Bursae
    4. Menisci
    5. Ligaments
  3. Hyphotheses
    1. Soccer
      1. Extensive kicking only with right leg when young
        1. Left knee may have undergone extensive rotational forces
      2. Findings so far
        1. Right leg
          1. Has more skilled muscular movements of the foot
          2. Some muscles seem to be more developed
      3. Possible therapy
        1. Gradually train the left leg
          1. Muscles required for kicking
          2. Skills training to train smaller muscles as well
    2. Drills
      1. Much greater stomping of left foot during drills

          I was in National Cadet Corps as a teenager and was extensively involved in drills. The stationary drills involved stomping of the left foot while tightening the muscles of the right leg for balance a lot more than the reverse.

      2. Possible links to discomfort
        1. May have injured some part of the left knee
        2. May have developed an overuse of right leg
          1. To support body weight
          2. To provide balance
      3. Possible therapy

          Reverse the stomping action may help to train the muscles of the left leg to in providing better support to the knee joint.

    3. Greater degree of flat footedness of left foot

        I performed a wet test and compared it to descriptions and with the results of my mother’s feet which are medium arched. I have a low arch on both feet but clearly, my left foot has a much lower arch. Almost the whole of the left foot appears in the wet test.

      1. Possible causes
        1. Not going bare foot enough
        2. Constantly walking on very flat surfaces
        3. Genetic
          1. Have to check my father’s as well first
      2. Possible therapy
        1. Barefoot runs/walks
          1. Along beach
          2. Along trails
          3. On grass
    4. Imbalance in strength of large muscles in left leg

        My left hamstring seems to be weaker than my right hamstring while both my quadraceps seem to be have about the same strength.

    5. Weak anterior crucialte ligament (ACL)

        The ACL stabilises the knee against hyper-extension and excessive rotation. I may have had an ACL injury that was not treated properly when I was young. I have memories of having fallen on my knees in the sand, resulting in a large wound in my left knee. It may have resulted in an ACL injury that was left untreated.

  4. Related issues
    1. Clicking sound in left knee cap

        I often get a clicking sound in my left knee cap when I bend my knees while they are loaded. For instance the clicking happens while getting into a squat from the standing position, but there is no clicking when simply bending at my knees while sitting down.

    2. Sitting cross legged
      1. Pain and clicking sound in left knee
        1. Only when sitting with left leg on the inside
    3. Right knee feels stronger and more in control
      1. Right knee has greater vibrational stability
      2. Right knee feels stronger
      3. Right knee seems to undergo far less rotation in daily activities

          Left knee seems to be subject to more rotational movements in my usual patten of daily motions.

    4. Ineffectiveness of conscious control of left leg motion
      1. Reasons
        1. The bio mechanics of leg motion are very complex
        2. Cannot control everything completely
        3. Possibly some of my muscles are weak
      2. Possible alternative
        1. Consciously focus only on where I want to go
        2. Let the legs and the sub-consciousness handle the leg motion
        3. Focus on strengthening the muscles instead
  5. Explore
    1. Effects of warmup on
      1. Bone
      2. Ligaments
      3. Cortisol
      4. Adrenaline
      5. Synovial membranes and synovial fluid
    2. Runner’s Knee
      1. How is it caused?

          Softening and wearing away of cartilage under the kneecap, resulting in pain and inflamation?

      2. Therapy
        1. Single leg squats

            May help to place one leg, bent right angle, on a stool. Gives more stability, allowing better focus on leg doing the squat.

          1. Strengthens quadriceps and hamstrings
        2. Walking lunges
          1. Strengthens quadriceps and hamstrings
    3. Iliotibial Band Syndrome
      1. Common thigh injury
      2. Leading cause of lateral knee pain in runners?
      3. Therapy
        1. Lateral squats
          1. Strengthens abuductors
