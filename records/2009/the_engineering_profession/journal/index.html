  22. 2009-10-05
    1. Added Question
      1. What should an employer look for when hiring/promoting?
        1. View BY Dee Hock, founder and CEO of Visa

            Hire and promote first on the basis of integrity; second, motivation; third, capacity; fourth, understanding; fifth, knowledge; and last and least, experience. Without integrity, motivation is dangerous; without motivation, capacity is impotent; without capacity, understanding is limited; without understanding, knowledge is meaningless; without knowledge, experience is blind. Experience is easy to provide and quickly put to good use by people with all the other qualities.

  23. 2009-09-16
    1. Concern
      1. Should I pursue a Master’s degree in Engineering?

          I had a chat with a Swedish exchange student. I appeared that in the European Union, all engineering courses were being standardised to 5 years and were direct Master’s degree courses.

          It also appears that in modernising countries, there are many, many people with Engineering Master’s degrees. I also sensed that the civil engineering graduate cohort was significantly larger than the undergraduate cohort.

          Moreover, my main inspiration for pursuing this degree is for people to trust me with “development work”.

          I am beginning to feel that under a ‘global’ contenxt, getting a Master’s degree will be a huge stepping stone for my engineering career. I enjoy what I am learning and I feel that at the end of my Bachelor’s degree I would still not have sufficient breadth and depth to be an all rounded Civil and Environemntal engineer.

          Still there are several issues that I wish to explore and address.
