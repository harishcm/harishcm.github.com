  3. 2009-08-14
    1. Activity
      1. Travelling to NUS from Dover MRT and Vice-Versa with inline skates
        1. Experience

            The distance and slopes are fine. Skating the route at night is also fine. The only difficult terrain is the flyover where the path is lined with uneven concrete drain covers.

            The pollution is more manageable as I need to breathe less when I am skating than when I am running. Still it is a concern.

  4. 2009-08-13
    1. Failure
      1. Seeking a less polluted route from NUS to Dover MRT

          I tried to find a route from the Sports and Recreation Centre across the Ayer Rajah Expressway and towards Dover MRT.

          The walking routes I tried all involve skirting around ITE College West, ACS Independent, United World College, and Singapore Polytechnic. This adds considerably to the distance.

          Further, the section of the route along the Ayer Rajah Expressway is moderately polluted. The fact that I need to get to the Sports and Recreation Centre from the Engineering Faculty first is a minor concern.

        1. Idea

            Possibly I could find routes to Buona Vista or Clementi Interchange instead. A longer distance is perfectly fine on inline skates or on a bicycle.

    2. Activity
      1. Travelling to NUS from Dover MRT by Running
        1. Inspiration

            I am pretty irritated with taking bus 96 from Clementi MRT to NUS. During the peak hours, it can easily take half-an-hour to get from Clementi MRT to NUS and vice-versa. Further, the amount of pollution at the Clementi MRT area is disturbing.

            I recognise that I can take bus 183 during the morning peak hours and achieve a shorter travel time. Still, it does not work during the evening peak hours.

        2. Experience

            It took me about 15min for the run from Dover MRT to NUS. This was with the heavily polluted path along Clementi Road, especially at the flyover just before reaching NUS.

            The distance is definitely feasible, but I do not want to experience the pollution.

        3. Thought

            Maybe skating the distance would feel better.

  5. 2009-05-27
    1. Activity
      1. Recce along the Southern Ridges for a skating route
        1. Experience

            The night before I decided to take a jog along the Southern Ridges series of parks and park connectors. The main aim was to recce for possible skating routes to NUS from the east. And I also wanted to get to NUS by 6.30pm, in time to play soccer with friends.

            It appears that with exception of the Marang Trail, the other sections are skatable. However, there are some sections requiring real skating proficiency - narrow slopes, handrail-less staircases, and pavement-less but low-traffic roads.

            Many sections of the path can get hot. So, planning for or carrying water will be great :) I completed the jog in 2h, but it really was not easy, with the slopes, heat, and a little traffic fumes at places.

            Maybe, someday, I will actually attempt an actual skate session :)
