Modelling a bank from set up to closure
=======================================
Part of the way banks work is through fractional reserve banking.
Basically, it means that only a small percentage of deposits is kept
with the bank, while the others are loaned out. This also means that
each time one takes a loan and spends it,
new money is being created in the economy.
However, there is definitely more to the ``story''.

For one, even with fractional reserve banking, there is still
a limit to how large the pool of money in an economy can grow.
Another gap in fractional reserve banking is that
deposits must precede loans. Another is that due to
interest being charged on loans, there will not be enough money in the economy
to repay all loans, unless brand new money
(not from fractional reserve banking) is created.

Do you guys have a clearer picture on how the banking/monetary system works?
I still have a lot of gaps in my understanding.
Two of my possible leads are "high-powered money" and "open market 
operations".
This video, [Money as Debt][MaD-vid], talks about

Some references that I have explored:

The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable BY Nassim Nicholas Taleb
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Talks about the role of risk and the inherent problems
with how we quantify risk. He says that we usually abuse
statistics, making rare events with huge consequences
seem a hell lot more rarer than they actually are.

Cybercash: The Coming Era of Electronic Money BY Robert Guttmann
----------------------------------------------------------------
Gives an account of what it takes/took in coming up with and managing
private/public forms of electronic money. Shows how many previous
attempts failed and problems faced in implementation and acceptance.
Gives quite a bit of history about the changes in the
banking/finance/money systems, their electronization,
increase in debt in the US over the years, the switch to credit money from
commodity money, innovations in money (tradable securities/derivatives)
and issues of regulation, etc. I have the book with me, can lend you guys!

Why economic theory is out of wack BY Mark Buchanan
---------------------------------------------------
Short article. I have attached it :) I did a couple of really really simple
programs simulating economies some time back. I am interested in
the kind of simulations they talk about.
