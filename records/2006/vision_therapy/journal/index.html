  7. 2006
    1. Explored Resource
      1. The Bates Method for Better Eyesight Without Glasses
        1. BY William Horatio Bates
        2. Book, 1971
        3. National Library Board, Singapore
        4. Initial Perception
          1. Accomodation

              Produced by extra-occular (outside of eye) muscles bending the cornea. Not the lens alone.

          2. There is a mental side to physiological viewing
          3. Causes for refractve errors
            1. Stress while viewing
            2. Seeing with effort

                Seeing is a passive activity. Seeing with effort causes refractive errors.

            3. Clearest vision not concentrated at center of visual field

                Only central vision should be clearest, while the rest of the visual field should be less clear. The fovea (central pit) region of the retina has the greatest concentration of cones. This corresponds to the center of the visual field.

          4. Therapy
            1. Remove your Spectacles
            2. Learn to relax while seeing as it is a passive process

                Close your eyes and place palms over eyes until view is totally dark.

            3. Make sure only the center of your vision is the clearest

                Practice motions of the eye—shifting and swinging.

                When eye stationary, vision is clear, when eye is moving vision must be blur (shifting).

                Moving your eyes one direction should cause things in non-central vision to move in the opposite direction (swinging).

            4. Practice reading a Snell Chart daily

                Imagine the object you want to see while trying to see it.

    2. Question
      1. What causes myopia?
        1. Optician

            When I was young, my optician said my eyeballs were growing longer.

        2. My mum

            Blamed it on viewing too much TV and computer monitors.

        3. Popular word

            Looking at the nearpoint for too long induces strain on lens muscles. They get stuck in ’near-point’ curvature. Far point sight becomes blur, nearpoint is still fine. Modern living makes us do lots of nearpoint work. Much higher chance of becoming myopic. People with good eysight are somehow lucky. Perhaps they are genetically more suited lenses/eyes?

          1. Comment 2009-08-04

              I think that people in the past did a lot of nearpoint work as well. Craftspeople are an easy example. Perhaps in these modern times, people in cities look at far away objects a lot less.
