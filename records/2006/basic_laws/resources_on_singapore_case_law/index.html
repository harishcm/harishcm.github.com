Your Question

Resources on Singapore Case Law

Our Reply

There are typically 6 types of tools to research about Singapore case law: (1)
Indexes to legal abbreviations, (2) Indexes to law reports, (3) Indexes with
abstracts of cases, (4) Citators, (5) Commentaries, and (6) Full-text
databases. Though NLB does not subscribe to case law online databases, it
offers the other tools which are mostly print resources.

Dear Harish,

LawNet is a service provided by the Singapore Academy of Law, and is the most
authoritative source of Singapore's legal information.

The National Library does not subscribe to LawNet nor any other similar
database. But we do have other research tools which are useful. Please see the
below resources. You may also wish to log on to the Online Catalogue ht tp://
vistaweb.nlb.gov.sg --> Search 'Law Singapore' --> Select 'Browse by Subjects'
--> Browse the other law publications.

If you are a library member with the National University of Singapore, you will
find their subscribed databases useful (in addition to LawNet - Legal
Workbench):
(1) LexisNexis
(2) Singapore & Malaysian Case Law Library
(3) Westlaw
