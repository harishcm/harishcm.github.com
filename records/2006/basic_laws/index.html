Basic Laws

  1. Inspiration
    1. Question

        What are some of the basic laws (in Singapore) that everyone should know?

  2. Aims
    1. Compile a list of these chosen laws
    2. Explain them in lay terms
    3. Include examples of how they have been interpreted in court
    4. Have links to resources for more information
  3. Extra Aims?
    1. Publish a book or website with this information?
    2. Translation project?
  4. People
    1. Pearlyn
      1. Introduced me to AG Chambers online law information
      2. Recommended me to read up about the constitution
      3. Told me there were many types of law
        1. What do I mean by ‘basic’?
        2. What types of law do I want to know about?
        3. If I were more specific she could answer me better
    2. Terry
    3. Wendy
  5. Explored Resources
    1. Singapore Statutes Online
      1. BY Attorney-General’s Chambers and Ministry of Finance, Singapore
      2. Online Database, Accessed Jul 2008
      3. URL

        Database of all laws currently in force in Singapore. However, it does not seem to have a historical record.

    2. You & the Law 3
      1. BY Singapore Association of Women Lawyers
      2. Book, 2002
      3. National Library Board, Singapore
      4. Initial Perception
        1. Too much technical details too early in my knowledge of Law
          1. Got me uninterested
          2. I was unable to swallow all of it
        2. Consitution is extremely important
          1. Provides direction for all laws and government policies
          2. Any law that does not adhere to constitution is void
    3. An Introduction to Singapore’s Constitution
      1. BY Kevin Y.L. Tan
      2. Book, 2005
      3. National Library Board, Singapore
      4. Initial Perception
        1. Laws and politics are intertwined
      5. Resource Recommendations
        1. Defending the Lion City
        2. A Citizen’s Guide to Government & Politics in Singapore
    4. Did You Say “Intellectual Property”? It’s a Seductive Mirage
      1. BY Richard Stallman
      2. Webpage, Accessed Apr 2008
      3. URL
      4. Initial Perception

          Talks about the unsuitablility of “Intellectual Property” as a term to collectively describe patents, copyrights and trademarks as it is distorting and misleading.

    5. Resources on Singapore Case Law
      1. BY Lam Yen Sze Elizabeth
      2. Question and Answer, Aug 2008

      4. Online Database, Accessed Aug 2008
      5. URL

      1. BY Manuel Monteagudo
      2. Journal Article
      3. IN Banking and Finance Law Review, Vol. 23, Pages 293-309
      4. Journal, 2008
    7. Laws That Choke Creativity
      1. BY Larry Lessig
