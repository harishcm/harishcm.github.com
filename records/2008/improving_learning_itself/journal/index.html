  12. 2010-01-03
    1. Idea

        Use machine speech software developed for blind persons for reading websites and e-books.

      1. Inspiration

          Reading on computer screens is straining. I also realise that it is considerably slower that reading from books. Technologies like e-ink are available, but the machines they come with are expensive, slow and crippled in what they can do.

          Once, while exploring web usability and accessibility, I saw a video of a blind person surfing the internet using a screen reader. The speed of the audio he was listening to was incredibly fast. He was likely to have been reading webpages faster than sighted people.

          This also represents a new means of interacting with computers and a possible avenue for social entrepreneurship.

  14. 2009-12-22
    1. Explored Resource
      1. The Surprising Science of Motivation
        1. BY Dan Pink
        2. Presentation, Jul 2009
        3. TEDGlobal 2009
        4. Conference
        5. Oxford, UK
        6. URL
      2. Initial Perception

          Extrinsic motivation significantly improves performance for deterministic problems with clear goals and rulesets. However, it significantly decreases performance for undeterministic problems with undefined goals and rulesets.

          In an experiment testing performace under an undeterministic problem, low and medium rewards achieved similar performance while high rewards significantly degraded performance.

          Autonomy, mastery and purpose were introduced as factors improving performance in problems requiring creativity and undeterministic problem solving.

      3. Explore

            Results Only Work Performance
