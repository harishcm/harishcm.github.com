    1. Wishcraft
      1. How to Get What You Really Want
      2. BY Barbera Sher with Annie Gottlieb
      3. Book, 1979
    2. Reading for the Plot, and Not Hearing the Story
      1. Ecological Tragedy and Heroic Capitalism
      2. BY Douglas Ezzy
      3. Book Article, 2001
      4. IN Utter Silence
      5. Voicing the Unspeakable
      6. EDITED BY Alice Mills and Jeremy Smith
      7. Book in Series, Vol. 59
      8. Initial Perception

          It was a great viewpoint on the fundamental difference in the ‘stories’ put forward by ecological and business/capitalist people.

    3. Language Lessons: You are what you speak
      1. BY Christine Kenneally
      2. Online Magazine Article, 01 Jun 2010
      3. IN New Scientist Magazine
      4. URL
      5. Initial Perception

          The article suggests that different languages develop the brain in different ways.

    4. The Simple, Ridiculously Useful Guide to Earning a Living from Your Passion
      1. BY Leo Babauta
      2. Online Article, Accesssed Dec 2010
      3. URL
      4. Initial Perception

          The part about starting out small, and doing favours for free, and then slowly charging small amounts as one’s skills improves was very interesting. Implementing some of the ideas in the article in my life and my learning would be interesting.

    5. How Do You Get to Carnegie Hall?
      1. 8 Keys to Deliberate Practice
      2. BY Jeff Cobb
      3. Online Article, 6 April 2010, Accessed Jan 2011
      4. URL
      5. Initial Perception

          The article discusses issues beyond the huge number of hours that one needs to put in to achieve mastery at specific tasks. Also introduces the concept of “flow”, or focus and full concentration in the specific task(s) at hand.

    6. Manufacturing Content
      1. BY Leo Babauta
      2. Blog Post, Accessed Dec 2010
      3. URL
      4. Initial Perception

          Provides a perspective that advertising for consumer goods is actually a very difficult job, and that just as several desires have been manufactured by careful, sustained advertising, content must be manufactured in ourselves and in society as well.

    7. The Truth Behind Opinion Polls
      1. BY BBC
      2. Comedy Show Segment
      3. IN The Ministerial Broadcast
      4. IN SERIES Yes Prime Minister, Series 1, Episode 2
      5. Segment Footage
      6. Initial Perception

          This was an excellent showcase of how questions in an opinion poll could be so as to make a respondent respond with either one of two diametrically opposite opinions on the same issue.

    8. The Black Swan
      1. The impact of the highly improbable
      2. BY Nassim Nicholas Taleb
      3. Book, 2007
      4. Notes
      5. Initial Perception

          It talks to some depth about different kinds of randomness and uncertainty that are around us, and why they cannot all be treated in the same way.

    9. How Facts Backfire
      1. Researchers discover a surprising threat to democracy: our brains
      2. BY Joe Keohane
      3. Online Newspaper Article, 11 July 2010, Accessed Aug 2011
      4. IN The Boston Globe
      5. URL
      6. Initial Perception

          An article highlighting research showing that when presented with facts refuting their beliefs, people tend to become more entrenched in their beliefs. Also gives a couple of possible solutions while noting that how to counter this tendency is still an open question.

    10. Breaking the Glass Ceiling Means Breaking Up With Your Middle School Mindset
      1. BY Lisa Gates
      2. Online Article, 1 Nov 2011, Accessed Nov 2011
      3. IN Forbes
      4. URL
      5. Initial Perception 2011-11-04

          The writer succinctly paraphrases another writer’s tips on mindset changes that are needed for many women if they wish to move into positions of authority. 

          - Getting comfortable with conflict, and learning how to resolve it.
- Separate tough business decisions from your self-judgement.
- As you achieve higher levels in your career, trade the female gold standard of over-delivering for relationship building, negotiating and influencing.
- Decide if you'd rather be liked or respected.
- Think about what YOU want.

          I felt that the tips were useful, regardless of gender.

    11. The Disciplined Pursuit of Less
      1. BY Greg McKeown
      2. Online Article, 8 Aug 2012, Accessed Aug 2012
      3. URL
      4. Initial Perception 2012-08-12

          I felt that the author’s suggestions were things that I should look into (modified from the article): 

          - Using more extreme criteria for what I want to invest time and effort into
- Asking myself what I really want and eliminating the rest
- Thinking of how much I would `pay' for something new, versus how much I would `pay' for what I already have
    12. Manage Your Energy, Not Your Time
      1. BY Tony Schwartz and Catherine McCarthy
      2. Article, October 2007
      3. IN Harvard Business Review
