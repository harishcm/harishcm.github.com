Failures

  1. Inspiration

      I have been intrigued by failures and people who have “failed” in their lives. It seems that failure is usually laughed at, shrugged off, or looked down upon. In academia it usually goes unreported. In the mass media, exceptional failures often make the news. Sometimes, failures are tapped upon to learn about workings of complicated systems (such as the brain, through people with cognitive defects; and in many fields of engineering, where investigations into accidents lead to new knowledge and changes to codes of practice).

      I feel that there is much to learn from failure. What intrigues me most is that learning from failure represents looking at issues from perspectives other than those deemed “successful”. One can then achieve a more holistic understanding of issues.
