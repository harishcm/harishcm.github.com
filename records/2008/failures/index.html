  2. 2010-01-09
    1. Flashing my water damaged phone from a Windows XP virtual machine

        My phone can connect through the usb port and get picked up in the virtual machine OS as well. However, only the SEMC Flash is detected and it only stays connected for a minute or less. Even the detection requires several attempts at plugging the usb connectors in and out before it gets detected.

        This may be a usb port power problem. The phone may need more power than usual because some of the circuits have become fried. Or maybe the hardware is too spoilt and I am on a hopeless adventure. Then perhaps it is time to attempt desoldering the flash chip and put it into a flash reader…

  3. 2010-01-02
    1. Flashing my water damaged phone from a Windows XP virtual machine

        This was pretty crazy. First, I started to read up about what flashing actually was and what tools there are out there. That took me almost an entire day. All the flashing software that I came across only worked on Windows, so I decided to install Windows through virtualization.

        For that I had to first create an iso image of the rescue dvd that came with my netbook. Finding out how to do that took a long time. In the end it was just a simple command using dd.

        Though that was settled, the rescue installer would not install on the virtual machine’s hard drive, and I had to modify the iso image to resemble a stock Windows XP disk and installer. After much trial and error, I finally realised that I could directly use one of the I386 folders in the image, while adding the files WIN51, WIN51C, WIN51C.SP1, WIN51C.SP2 and WIN51C.SP3, and getting a suitable boot image from the internet.

        After installation in the virtual machine I got internet running on the virtual machine and downloaded the phone software updater from Sony Ericsson. Only then did I realise that I could not directly connect my damaged phone through usb to the virtual machine in an usual fashion.

        By then I was too tired to go further. 3 days of trying to solve the problem was a bit too much.

        On a brighter note, I got a feel of virtualization. And I am glad for that. I am likely to use virtualization pretty often from now on. Using more than one OS at the same time felt really good.

  6. 2008-10-22
    1. Patching the A-bicycle tire puncture

        Itended to spend a couple of hours in the morning to patch up the inner tube of my A-bicycle that I had damaged by pumping in too much air or had spoilt due to wear and tear/inferior quality.

        Then realised that I could not turn most of the hexagonal socket screws found in the bicycle as I did not the hex keys of the correct size. Mine were slightly too big or slightly too small and were also not slightly tapered as the hexagonal sockets appeared to be.

        I also realised, again as I had removed the rear wheel once before also in attempting to replace the inner tube, that I could not unscrew the rear break holder. I had to instead unscrew the break wire to give space to remove the wheel.

        After several hours, I managed to remove the rear wheel, remove the inner tube, find the punctures, patch them and reassemble everything. I even found that the rear wheel could be pumped to a higher pressure, which meant a smoother ride, when the tire was raised slightly where the air valve was located.

        I then tried out the bike within my house. After a few rounds, I heard this familiar “pisst” sound, the same one that I heard when the tire initially got punctured.

        A new problem with a lock spring getting stuck to the chain, preventing the bike from being opened also appeared and is still unsolved, though the problem area has been identified.

        I did not find the persistence to continue on that day. I had also planned to use the remaining of the day to prepare for a school test the next day.

        My reactions were mixed, considering on one hand that I had spent several hours trying to fix it, and on the other, that it was a lesson in failure, that just because I had worked hard, I was not entitled to “success”. But I got to see how things could be slowed down greatly when the required tools were unavailable and got valuable experience in fixing a puncture/changing an inner tube.

Maybe I’ll email the farmers to inform them that I want to come down next Wednesday.

  8. 2008-04-20
    1. Apache DAV Setup

        Tried to set up Apache WebDAV so that I get a friend to upload files to my computer without problems with his firewall. I followed a short set up process but when I accessed the WebDAV URL, it showed Forbidden. I assumed that there was something wrong with the set-up I just did and tried randomly changing stuff to see if it would work. Nothing did.

        Eventually I found that URLs to all my other folders that did not have an index.html file in them gave the same error. The Forbidden code was to do with me main Apache server setup! Darn. And I had rolled back all the changes I had made to set up WebDAV.

        I guess it is a lesson to learn more about what I am setting up instead of blindly following instructions.
