  5. Model Websites
    1. Eric S. Raymond’s Home Page

        Eric S. Raymond’s Personal Website. He is one of the pioneers of the internet and his website is an excellent model for what I want to achieve.

    2. Fourmilab

        Website of John Walker, co-writer of AutoCAD. May be another site I can model mine upon. His website contains a collection of personal writings and his reading list.

    3. The Autodidact Project

        A personal site by Ralph Dumain. It’s something similar to something I want to do, and has many resources for visitors as well.

    4. Daring Fireball

        I love the simplicity combined with elegance, content and standards adhereance of this website by John Grubber.

    5. Get Rich Slowly

        Blog by J.D. Roth who is learning about personal finance. I could learn from the way the site is organised.

    6. GIMP Plugin Registry

        Gimp plugin registry. An inspiration for my website?

    7. Winnie’sSite

        Beautiful site created with Ikiwiki.

    8. Google.org

        Clean, simple, purposeful and usable focused yet beautiful. Gives only as little information as asked. Another model website.

    9. Noel’s Lab

        Website created entirely in a single otl file. The file was later split by otlsplit and htmlized by olt2html.

        I did not like the resulting html document as it has no heading tags. Not good for search engine placement and general structure. Also did not see the point of using numbered lists for all outlines and css to modify the look to an unordered list or simple list.

        However, looks good and reads well in a text browser. Numbered Lists add readability as they show where you are in the document. Plus it might make sense that headings for outlines are not necessary. Or I could just nest appropriate header tags under list item tags.

    10. Global Issues

        I could learn from the organization of the site and the usage of citations in the html as the separators of content.

    11. Wikipedia

        Exploring the structure and organization of the encyclopedia will be valuable. I can learn from the disambiguation pages.
