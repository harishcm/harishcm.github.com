Subject: Advice regarding analysing and presenting large amounts of data

Hello Jared,

This is Harish. Nice to have met you at the Global Social Innovators 
Forum! I apologise for not getting to the point quickly during our 
conversation, haha!

Actually, I wanted to ask you for advice regarding analysing and 
presenting large amounts of data. I kind of want to be able to get a 
feel for social/economic/environmental type of data from the UN and 
other governments/organisations.

An example I found was charts that are animated over the time domain. 
Additionally, colours and data point sizes could represent variables 
other than those on the two axes. A real example would be 
[Gapminder][gap]. Gapminder is in fact part of a movement for good 
availability and representation of data related to social issues. I 
think this [talk][talk] by Hans Rosling made during the 2006 TED 
Conference explains it well.

[gap]: http://www.gapminder.org/world
[talk]: 
http://www.gapminder.org/videos/ted-talks/hans-rosling-ted-2006-debunking-myths-about-the-third-world/

What are some other ways that you may recommend to me for analysing 
large amounts of data, especially from the social realm? And also, 
how useful have you ``found official'' statistics to have been, 
especially in understanding issues that are close to your heart. Say 
regarding the life and challenges of migrant workers in Singapore? :)

Thanks for the help and wish you a nice day!

Best regards,
