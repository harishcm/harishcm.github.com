Statistics Versus Preconceptions

  1. Inspiration

      Oftentimes, when I read statistics, I get surprised. There is more than enough grain alone produced around the world to feed more than twice the world population (Source: World Hunger 12 Myths). You are not going to get rich as a drug dealer unless you are at the very top of the operation (Source: Freakonomics).

      Hans Rosling’s Talk: Debunking Third-World Myths With The Best Stats You’ve Ever Seen at the 2006 TED Conference amazed me at how ignorant I and others were about changes that have been happening in the Third World. I have also heard from Ben that doctors (these days?) base their diagnosis largely on statistics.

      So I want to get hold of statistics in as many domains as I can, so as to get a better picture of the world around me.
