What made me interested in this internship/apprenticeship
---------------------------------------------------------

I have been interested in food issues for about 2 years or so now.
It started off when I picked up this book, World Hunger: 12 Myths
by Frances Moore Lappe and Joe Collins. It made me realised that
World Hunger is an extremely interconnected complex topic, with 
links from ``modes of production'' to international trade to
international finance to local social/economic/climate conditions 
and cultures.

I then went on to read a little about monocropping, pesticide usage
and some of its long term effects, humanity's culture of seed banking 
and sharing, etc. I have also read a little and seen videos about 
industrial agriculture, along with it's inherent cruelty, huge energy 
and water usages and huge subsidies (in the US and the EU). Recently,
I watched this documentary about genetically engineered crops--- 
The World According to Monsanto by Marie-Monique Robin. 

I guess much of my readings have been heavily biased towards viewpoints
that highlight the problems with food and agriculture around the world.
Only a small part of what I read was about projects to solve these issues,
and about viewpoints from those in favour of the status quo. 
Also much of my readings have been US-centric.

Learning about and having some experience in organic farming right
at home in Singapore are a natural extension to learning pursuits 
regarding food and agriculture :)

Other related interests
-----------------------

I am greatly interested in getting a feel of the 
``data and statistics'' regarding food and other issues. I am 
currently learning a little about data visualisation---representing 
data in visual and software forms so as to make it more accessible 
to people and to be able to see it from different perspectives.

I am also doing some research into `soap plants' for the 
World Toilet Organisation. These are plants that can or have been 
used as direct soap substitutes. It is mainly to benefit rural/urban
poor who do not buy soap. If `soap plants' can be grown next to their
washing areas, it would be a bonus to sanitation efforts around the
world. I have done a short proof of concept so far. Do ask me for it 
if it interests you :)
