Organic Farming

  1. Inspiration

      I got more and more interested with the idea of seeing for myself what happens at a ‘real’ farm.

      Along the way, I got to talk to one of my uncles, who was a farm land owner in India. It was enlightening when I realised that he was now growing rice as a cash crop, and ate “better quality” rice, imported from another state. His main buyer was the government, whom he said paid them well for their produce.

      I then came across several other articles on humus and biochar. Also, Heather brought up the idea of youngsters interning or doing an apprenticeship on a local organic farm. I had also been doing research on “soap plants” for the World Toilet Organisation.

      I felt it kind of made sense—to organise many of these related efforts and occurances into an ad-hoc interest.
