Subject: RE: Modules covering ``using local materials'' for building and construction

Dear Prof Zhang,

Thanks for the example of recycling concrete from demolished materials. Sorry I did not explain what I meant by `local materials'. Maybe a better phrase would be ``locally appropriate materials''.

As an example, many communities lack access to sufficient amounts of steel and concrete. Recent price increases in these materials exacerbate this situation. So for development, alternate building materials have to be found. It is even likely that these communities have had less costly traditional building methods which were safe and well suited for their situation.

Some locally appropriate building materials I have come across are adobe bricks, rammed earth, compressed earth, lime/cement stabilization, bamboo structures, rebar roof truss, etc. My source is mainly this book, //Design like you give a Damn//, edited by Architecture for Humanity. It gives examples of Architectural responses to humanitarian crises and development needs in rural and marginalised areas.

Are there NUS modules which cover such ``locally appropriate materials'' and related building and sourcing considerations? Are there other forms of learning opportunities in this area?

I hope I have been clearer :) Thank you for your assistance Prof Zhang!

Regards,
Harish
