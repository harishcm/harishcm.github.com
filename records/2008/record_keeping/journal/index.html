      1. Better Backup Mechanisms
        1. Inspiration

            It is very cumbersome and error prone to transfer files one by one into the back-up media. I want to automate the process of changes being backing up.

        2. Considerations
          1. With a Versioning Repository
          2. Without a Versioning Repository

  11. 2009-02
    1. Possible Further Improvements
      1. Storing Records in a Versioning Repository
        1. Possible Repositories
          1. SVN
          2. Git
          3. CVS

              Cannot save state of complete directories. SVN was specifically built to improve on CVS.

        2. Benefits
          1. History of Changes

              Versioning repositories can store when and how files were changed and provide facilities to revert to previous changes or retrieve the state of documents or folders at a time in the the past.

          2. End of line (EOL) character portability
            1. SVN Solution

                SVN allows one to check out text files with EOL characters used by the user’s operating system. However, I might then need to have a working internet connection. I could keep a copy of the head version of the data at all times and edit to it though.

            2. Other Solutions?
  12. 2008-11
    1. New Records Format and Method
      1. VimOutliner Outline Files
        1. Similarities
          1. Text files
          2. Readable
          3. Quick editing
        2. Differences
          1. Outline levels differentiation

              Tab characters instead of spaces.

          2. Header and plain text differentiation

              Plain text is indicated by prefixing a colon and a space or by a leading space.

        3. Benefits
          1. Colour hilighting of outline levels and plain text
          2. Minimal adjusting required
          3. Folding and unfolding of content
          4. Keystrokes are very fast (2-3 keys per command)
          5. Differentiation between headers and normal text
          6. Ability to promote or demote many lines of text at one go
          7. Uses Vim
            1. Available on different platforms
            2. Portable versions available

  23. 2007
    1. Added to records
      1. Record Keeping

          Records are kept of ad-hoc interests, learning projects and resources.

      2. Purpose
        1. Serves to compile learning pursuits and achievements

            Enabless these learning pursuits and achievements to be stored and retrieved for a much longer time than they will reamin in my memory.

        2. Enables better evaluation and criticism

            By me and by others.

        3. Enhances the learning process

            Much thought and organisation is required when penning down ideas formulated.

      3. Format for records
        1. Criteria
          1. Easy for visual scanning

              Facilitate quick information retrieval.

          2. Flexible

              A large variety of pursuits and achievements are to be recorded.

          3. Concise
        2. Current format
          1. Each point occupies its own line
          2. Sub-points are indented to the right
          3. Joining words are used where it improves readability
          4. Headings are used with a Semicolon at the end

              To distinguish them from non-headings and reduce the number of indentations required.

      4. Records storage
        1. Digital records are kept in text files

            So that they are platform independent, software independent and take up very little disk space.

      5. Current Push
        1. Format

            Making the flow better so that it facilitates both visual scanning and actual reading.
