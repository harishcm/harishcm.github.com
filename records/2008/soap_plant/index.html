    5. Limitations of soap
      1. Ineffective in acidic water

          Soaps are salts of weak acids. Acidic conditions cause soaps to be converted to fatty acids. Fatty acids are less soluble in water and will form precipitates (soap scum), making soaps ineffective in acidic water.

      2. Ineffective in hard water

          Hard water is water containing dissolved minerals such as magnesium, calcium and iron. Soap reacts with these ions to form insoluble salts, forming precipitates. This makes soaps ineffective in hard water.
