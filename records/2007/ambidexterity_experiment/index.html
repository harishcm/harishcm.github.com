Ambidexterity Experiment

  1. Inspiration

      In junior college, I was impressed by Eugene Koh’s ability to write fairly well with his non-muster hand. That made me feel that I could do so just as well.

      It happened that I found filling in the blanks or copying lecture notes during lectures useless and stupid. So I decided to do so with my non-muster hand, to make the time useful.

  2. Hypothesis
    1. Muster handedness is ‘fake’
      1. It just happends that people adapt to using
        1. One hand for some action and
        2. The other for another action
      2. Both hands usually work complimentary to one another
      3. It’s more mental than physical
  3. Training opportunities
    1. Housework
      1. Washing dishes
      2. Sweeping the floor
      3. Mopping
    2. Writing
      1. Largely successful
        1. Latin alphabet

            I currently seem to write like a 10–12 year old kid.

      2. Still lacking
        1. Tamil alphabet
      3. Next steps
        1. Improving speed and stability
        2. Handling curved forms better
        3. Writing more in the Tamil alphabet
        4. Being able to sign with non-muster hand
    3. Brushing teeth
      1. Largely successful
        1. Moving in circles
        2. Adequate force
      2. Next steps
        1. Improving the circular motion
    4. Badminton
      1. Fairly successful
        1. Can play with kids
      2. Next steps
        1. More practice
        2. Being able to
          1. Hit from the back court well
          2. Feel comfortable
          3. Have more learned reflexes
    5. Throwing
      1. Seems to be more difficult than writing
    6. Digging with spade / hoe
      1. Largely successful
        1. Able to alternate sides
        2. Balanced fatigue on both sides of body
        3. Tire out less easily
  4. Physical changes

      Initially my left shoulder bones seemed to be unfused and were smaller than my right shoulder bones. Right now, they seem to have somewhat fused and seem to have increased in size.

  5. Behavioural changes
    1. I write almost exclusively with my non-muster hand now
      1. Signature is still with my muster hand
  6. Mental changes

      I try to do everything trying to feel that both my hands are equally skillful or my left hand is the muster hand. Sometimes I feel a slight fear of not being able to accomplish a job with my left hand.
