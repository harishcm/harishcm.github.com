Framework

  1. Components
    1. Ad-hoc Interests
    2. Learning Projects
    3. Record Keeping
    4. Improving Learning Itself
  2. Ad-hoc Interests

      The basis for self-directed learning.

    1. Examples

      Thoughts, questions, ideas, comments that one gets interested in from time to time.

    2. Purpose
      1. Start off Learning Activities / Pursuits and
      2. Keep them going
    3. Philosophy

        Learning is initiated and developed intrinsically while resources and guidance from the outside are enthusiastically sought after.

    4. Suppression of Ad-hoc Interests

        Unfortunately ad-hoc interests are usually suppressed as we feel they are not important, not worth the time, or that other activities should be allocated the time instead (especially school / work).

    5. Importance

        Ad-hoc interests are individually small but collectively they define one’s individuality. Ad-hoc give direction in setting one’s plans and priorities. They are also starting points for cultivating special skills and talents.

  3. Learning Projects

      Centrepiece of self-directed learning. Form the core of my self-directed learning.

    1. Purpose
      1. Organize and Document my Learning Pursuits
      2. Set Direction / Priorities in my Learning
    2. Begining

        Learning project are started off from ad-hoc interests which have developed into pursuits with specific aims & learning plans.

    3. Continuation

        They continue on like as an investigative process and are generally not bounded by depth or breadth.

    4. Evolution

        As a learning project evolves however, it may be split into two or more branches or merged with other learning projects.

      1. Purpose

          To ensure that learning projects remain flexible yet organized.

    5. Dormant Learning Projects
      1. Usually left open ended
        1. Symbolism that learning never ends
        2. For Future Pursuits
      2. But may reach a conclusion and ending if deemed appropriate
    6. Records
      1. Instrumental Part of all Learning Projects
      2. Special attention paid to documenting
        1. The Investigative Trail
        2. Resources Seeked
        3. Keywords Used
        4. Thoughts Gathered &
        5. Reflections which lead to modified strategies
  4. Purposes of Framework
    1. Give Self-directed Learning a Structure
      1. Learning Activities / Pursuits can be Organized
      2. Progress / Results can be clearly seen
      3. Credibility can be ascertained
      4. Priorities can be highlighted
      5. Structure and Procedures Give ’Official’ Status
  5. Want
    1. Structure
      1. Framework
    2. Procedures
      1. Carrying around writing materials
      2. Firm daily Habits
      3. Promotion from Ad-hoc Interest to Learning Project
    3. Put things in Writing
      1. Records
    4. Display and present what you are doing nicely / formally
      1. Have printed documents
      2. Have a well designed and well maintained website
