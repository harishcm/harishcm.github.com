Learning Languages

  1. Initial Inspiration

      I am pissed off with the paradigm/notion that children are more capable than adults at learning languages. I want to prove the statement wrong.

  2. Initial Hypothesis

      Adults can do the same if barriers to natural learning are broken.

  3. Current Inspirations
    1. Compliment the push to know more about Southeast Asia
    2. Learning languages will be something novel
      1. Unlike my other learning pursuits
      2. Likely to greatly improve my learning skills and resources
      3. Will be a challenge
    3. The older Singaporean adults can speak several languages and dialects
      1. Malay
      2. English
      3. Mandarin or Tamil
      4. Other Chinese dialects

        If they could, so can I!

    4. Feel that it is natural to do so
    5. I live in a multi-lingual society

        It is a waste to not use the avaiable resources

  4. Possible techniques
    1. Hear native speakers
      1. Online Podcasts ?
    2. Audio books
    3. Software
    4. Help with open source software translations
  5. Possible steps
    1. Find out basic subject/verb/object order in language
    2. Learn a certain number of common words in a language
      1. Explore
        1. How many words are actually used in day to day speech
    3. Learn to use the language dictionary
  6. Explore
    1. Standard word lists
    2. Syllabus/structure of language courses
    3. How we learn languages
      1. When young
      2. Informally
      3. Formally
  7. Languages I know
    1. English
    2. Tamil
      1. Want
        1. Make it a habit to read Tamil books
          1. Especially non-fiction
        2. Write more essays/letters/postings/etc. in Tamil
        3. Speak to my Tamil friends in Tamil more often
        4. Design or contribute to a Tamil Font
  8. Languages I want to learn next
    1. Malay
      1. Inspirations

          Variants of Malay are widely spoken in Singapore and in neighbouring Malaysia and Indonesia. Most older Singaporeans can speak Malay and we are very culturally, geographically and economically intertwined.

          Also, during my national service days I got to know more freinds and acquaintances who could speak Malay. That left me with an elevated interest to learn the language.

      2. Possible Resources
        1. Kamus Bahasa Melayu-Inggeris
          1. Malay-English Cyber-Dictionary
          2. BY Dr Bhanot
          3. Online Dictionary, Accessed June 2009
          4. URL
          5. Initial Perception
            1. Seems only Malay words can be searched for
    2. Mandarin
      1. Inspiration

          I already have a limited vocabulary and a ‘feel’ for the basics of the language. It makes sense to improve on it. Further most Singaporeans can speak Mandarin and I will have many friends/acquaintances to speak to.

  9. Other languages in mind
    1. Thai
      1. Inspiration

          Partly it is due to the push to learn more about the Southeast Asian region. I also feel that Thais are proud of their culture. Thailand is also unique in that it was not colonised by any of the imperial powers during the past few centuries. I also feel that Singaporeans may have much to learn from the civil society in Thailand.

    2. Swedish
      1. Inspiration

          I wanted to know more about Scandinavian countries. It will help if I go on internships or exchange programs there.

    2. Creole languages
      1. Esperanto
    3. Visual languages
      1. Blissymbols
    4. International Phonetic Alphabet
