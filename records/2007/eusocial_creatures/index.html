    1. Terminte Nests
      1. Temperature control through geometry and orientation

          Magnetic Termite mounds are wedge shaped and the wide sides face east-west. In the morning, the eastern side is warmed while the western side remains cool. At noon only the wedge-tip is warmed. And in the afternoon and evening, the western side is warmed while the eastern side cools.

      2. Ventilation of underground tunnels through air-exchange mounds

          Warm air from underground tunnels rises up a conical mound through a central vent. However, it cannot escape directly as the mound top is air tight. The sides of the mound are permeable and allow the wind to bring in cool air from the outside. The air current around the mound creates a low pressure region on the downwind side. This sucks the warm air out from the central vent.
