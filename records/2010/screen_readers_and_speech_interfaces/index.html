Screen Readers and Speech Interfaces

  1. Inspiration
    1. Strain and low productivity from reading text on computer screens

        I read a lot of text on my computer. Unfortunately, it is more straining that reading text on paper. This seems to make me a slower reader, and it contributes to unnecessary strain.

    2. Realising visually impaired persons use computers comfortably

        I had once seen a video of a visually impaired person surfing the internet. He was using a screenreader to read out the text that appeared on the screen. The speed at which he was listening to the text being read out was amazing—way too fast for me to comprehend anything. He also was very comfortable working with other computer software such as those used in document preparation and managing emails.

    3. So I figured it was time to learn how visually impaired persons use computers
