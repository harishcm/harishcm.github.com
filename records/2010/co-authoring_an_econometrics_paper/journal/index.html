      1. Saving ourselves from the brink: The use of safety factors in economic policy and management

          In engineering design, safety factors are commonly introduced to compensate for varibility and uncertainty. Sometimes //Reliability Analysis// is used to deal with uncertainties.

          There are at least 3 types of uncertainty recognised in engineering: inherent uncertainty, statistical uncertainty, and model uncertainty. Additionally, uncertainty can also be categorized as ontological uncertainty (fundamental randomness) or epistemological uncertainty (lack of knowledge).

          In any case, engineering already has the tools and methods for dealing with uncertainty in rational ways, which have proved successful over time. It would be interesting to apply such tools and methods to economics, especially since much data is available.
