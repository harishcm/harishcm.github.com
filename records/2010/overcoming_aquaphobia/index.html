Overcoming Aquaphobia

  1. Inspiration

      I can now swim laps of the swimming pool using breaststroke. It often feels uncomfortable, but it gets better as I train. With correct knowledge and training, I know that I am capable of so much more.

  2. Background

      I have been afraid of water since I was very young. Both my parents could not swim, and thus they did not take me to the pool either. At that time, I was also very afraid of things that were unknown and seldom tried out new things.

      Once, when I was about 5 years old, I was brought to be pool by my mum and the lady who was staying in my house as a tenant. When introduced to the water by my tenant, I was holding onto her arm tightly, refusing to let go. After extended encouragement, I was finally made to let go. Upon letting go, I panicked and struggled. Water got into my nose. It felt like I was drowning. And that was the end of swimming for a long time, up until swimming lessons in secondary school (12 years of age).

      In secondary school, there were compulsory swimming lessons for everyone, especially because the swimming pool was a 5 minute jog from the school compound. It was a traumatic experience, accompanied with embarrassment. After 10 weeks, while most of the others passed, I was just putting my head into the water. Those who did not pass had to do a re-course. This time around there were only a handful of students, and the coach was much more supportive. By the end of the second 10 weeks, I could do 3 single strokes of the breaststroke, but I could not tread water. Looking back at those days always brings a smile.

      Over the years, my friends have helped me out in improving my swimming. Eugene Koh, Priscilla, Saravanan and Kin Fei were instrumental in providing with encouragement, support, and positive peer pressure in bettering my swimming. A three month stint as a shallow water lifeguard at a water theme park improved my confidence quite a bit. Without my friends, I would have never thought of attempting the stint at the water theme park.

      My greatest achievement so far has been to swim about 300–400m in calm open water. I was surrounded by friends assuring me that they would save me if I were to drown.

  3. Current Status
    1. Fairly comfortable with eyes in water
  4. Possible Approaches

    2. Get comfortable doing simple things in water
      1. Facing downwards and rolling the head from side to side
      2. Opening and closing the mouth while in water
    3. Get comfortable with floating on back
  5. Strokes I am Interested In
    1. Front crawl
    2. Sidestroke
    3. Backstroke
  6. Techniques I am Interested In
