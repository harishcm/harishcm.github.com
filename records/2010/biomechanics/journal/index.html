Biomechanics: Journal

  1. 2012-09-15
    1. Added Explored Resource
      1. Your Body, Your Voice
        1. The key to natural singing and speaking
        2. BY Theodore Dimon
        3. Book, 2011
        4. Initial Perception

            A book for the mass market created from a research thesis into human voice production. I really like the point that an upright posture is the fundamental prerequisite for effortless voice production, and that the simple way to achieve the posture is to tilt the head lower so that the spine, tendons and muscles are able to pull on each other and achieve a stable tensile structure—similar to how a tent works.

  2. 2011-10-31
    1. Realisation

        It is probable that posture essentially means resting on your bones and skeleton when being stationary.

        During my rifle marksmanship lessons in National Service, I was taught that good posture while firing a rifle meant that I had to rest on my bones and skeleton, instead of relying on my muscles to maintain the posture.

        This was especially so when I had to remain stationary for long periods of time in anticipation of taking a shot. If I were to rest on my muscles, I would not be able to maintain the firing posture for more than a minute or so. If one were to rest on my bones and use the skeleton as a frame instead, I could actually stay still, holding the weight of the rifle, and with the target in sight, for a much much longer period of time.

        I wonder how much of the above applies to posture, while moving.
