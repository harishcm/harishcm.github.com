Biomechanics

  1. Inspiration

      I have long been interested in sports and fitness. In recent years I also got interested in human and sometimes animal locomotion, gait, posture, anatomy, physiology, martial art, and so on.

      I realise that there is a wealth of information available, and one only needs to spend the time, effort and resources to become so much more well informed about ourselves and proficient at using our body.

      1. Yoga

          Mind-body approach through asanas (various postures) and breathing.

      2. Chiropractic

          Aligning spinal bones.

      3. Massage therapy

          Relaxing muscles with massage.

      4. Pilates

          Core strengthening with a mind-body approach.

      5. Feldenkrais

          Changes the way our brain interprets specific movements so as to release habits that cause pain and tension, and replace them with those aligned with natural design of our body. Focuses on micro-movements.

      6. Franklin method

          Using imagery of body movements to ease posture and body motion. Aims to make one an expert on doing the things one spends most of their time doing: sitting, standing, lying down or walking.

      7. Manual medicine

          Using our hands to mobilize and stabilize our spine.

      8. Tai Chi

          Combination of movement and meditation for calmness, flexibility and stability.

      9. Alexander technique

          Learning to decompress one’s spine in a holistic manner.
